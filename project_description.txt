1. Project Overview	
   - What is the main purpose or goal of this project?	The main purpose of this project is to provide technical students with a practical opportunity to design and implement innovative digital solutions tailored to the needs of a modern dance school. The goal is to improve operational efficiency, enhance student and parent experience, and streamline administrative processes through the use of digital technologies.
   - Who will use this project or product, and why do they need it?	"This project will be used by several key groups within the dance school community, each benefiting in specific ways:

Students (Dancers):
Dancers will benefit from features such as live class broadcasts, allowing them to participate remotely when they cannot attend in person. The digital access system will make entering and exiting the building more convenient and streamlined, while also ensuring accurate attendance tracking. This creates a smoother and more flexible experience for students.

Parents:
Parents will have the opportunity to observe their children's classes in real time through the live broadcasts, helping them stay informed and involved in their child’s progress. This level of transparency increases trust and engagement between the school and families.

Teachers:
Teachers will find their work simplified with automated attendance records, real-time class streaming (eliminating the need to personally update absent students), and a more organized costume system. This allows them to focus more on teaching and less on administrative tasks.

Principal and Administrative Staff:
The principal and staff will benefit significantly from the digitalization of access control, attendance, costume management, and registration systems. These tools reduce manual work, prevent errors, and provide clear records of who attended classes, which costumes are in use, and which students are enrolled. This makes daily operations more efficient and data-driven.

In summary, this product is designed to serve the entire dance school ecosystem. It addresses practical challenges and improves workflow efficiency, while also enhancing the overall user experience. Most importantly, it helps make the work of both the teachers and the principal easier by automating routine tasks and centralizing key processes."
   - What business problems or needs are we aiming to solve?	"This project aims to solve several practical and operational problems currently faced by the dance school, as well as to meet key organizational and communication needs. These include:

1. Limited Access for Remote or Absent Participants:
   One major challenge is the inability of some students to attend classes in person due to illness, scheduling conflicts, or transportation issues. By introducing live video broadcasting from each studio, students can still follow classes remotely, helping maintain learning consistency and engagement.

2. Lack of Transparency for Parents:
   Currently, parents have little visibility into what happens during classes. This can lead to questions or concerns about the quality of instruction or their child’s participation. With live video access at specific times, parents can observe the structure and content of the classes, increasing their confidence and trust in the school.

3. Manual and Inefficient Attendance Tracking:
   Attendance is often tracked manually or through basic systems that require extra administrative work. The digital access system will automate this process, reducing errors and ensuring accurate, real-time attendance data that can be used for reporting and planning.

4. Inefficient Costume Management:
   Costumes are essential for performances, but tracking which student has borrowed which costume, and whether it has been returned, is often done manually or not at all. A barcode-based costume system will allow for fast and accurate check-out and return logging, preventing loss and improving accountability.

5. Difficult Class Registration and Payment Process:
   The current system for signing up and paying for classes may be unclear, outdated, or too dependent on manual processes. By offering a user-friendly website with links to a registration and payment platform (Wix), the process becomes easier, more efficient, and more professional for both users and staff.

6. Administrative Overload:
   Managing daily operations takes up a lot of time and effort from both the principal and the teachers. By automating key systems (access, attendance, costume tracking, registration), the workload is reduced significantly, allowing the staff to focus more on teaching and improving the school’s educational offerings.

In short, this project addresses real business needs, improving efficiency, transparency, user satisfaction, and administrative control, while also supporting the growth and modernization of the dance school."
   - Are there any similar projects or solutions we can reference? Something you already have?	"Yes, the dance school already has a few existing systems and tools that serve as a foundation for this project. While they are limited in scope and functionality, they demonstrate that some digital elements are already in place. These include:

Cameras in Studios (Current Use):
Cameras are already installed in each studio, but they are currently used only for internal monitoring by the school owner. Their main purpose is for security, such as checking for robberies or monitoring behavior in case of any incidents. These cameras are not currently used for educational purposes or live streaming to students or parents.

Two Existing Websites:
The dance school currently operates two websites. These sites already provide general information and act as a digital presence for the school. The only feature missing is an integrated registration form with an option to pay for classes online. This feature is part of the new project and will enhance the functionality of the existing websites.

Digital Costume File (in Excel):
The school maintains a costume inventory in an Excel file, which includes each costume's number and a photo. This file is helpful but requires manual updates and doesn’t allow for real-time tracking of costume distribution or returns. The project aims to transform this static file into an interactive barcode-based digital costume management system for better efficiency and traceability."
	
2. Scope and Goals	
   - What are the main things you expect this project to accomplish?	"We expect this project to achieve the following key goals:

1. Enable remote participation by live-streaming classes for students who can’t attend in person and for parents who want to observe their child’s progress.

2. Simplify access and attendance tracking with a QR code (for example) system that records entry and exit times automatically.

3. Improve costume management through a barcode system that tracks which student has each costume and when it’s returned.

4. Streamline class registration and payments by adding an online form with payment options to the existing website.

5. Reduce the administrative workload for teachers and the principal by automating routine tasks and organizing information digitally."
   - What specific tasks or features are essential for the project to succeed?	"To ensure the success of the project, the following tasks and features are essential:

Live Streaming Setup:
Configure cameras to securely stream classes at scheduled times, with access for students and parents.

QR Code Access System:
Develop a system that allows dancers to enter and exit using a phone-scanned QR code, automatically logging attendance.

Barcode Costume Tracking:
Create a digital system that assigns costumes to students using barcodes, recording when they are issued and returned.

Website Integration for Registration and Payment:
Add a user-friendly form to the existing website that allows online registration, with or without immediate payment.

Data Management and Reporting Tools:
Build a simple interface to manage attendance records, costume inventory, and registration data in one place.

Each of these components is necessary to meet the project's goals and provide real value to the school’s students, staff, and parents."
   - Is there anything that should not be included in this project?	"Yes, the project should avoid the following:

Physical access tools like key cards or chips:
The goal is to keep the system fully digital and accessible via smartphones to reduce costs and simplify use.

Complex or expensive hardware installations:
The focus is on practical, cost-effective solutions using existing infrastructure where possible.

Unnecessary features not related to the dance school’s daily operations:
The project should stay focused on real needs, like streaming, access control, costume tracking, and registration, and avoid extras that would add complexity without clear benefit."
